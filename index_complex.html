Currently, I am a first year CS PhD student working with Professor Svetlana Lazebnik at University of Illinois (UIUC) in Computer Vision Group @ UIUC. Before that, I spent wonderful two years with Professors Robert Collins and Yanxi Liu in Laboratory for Peception, Action and Cognition (LPAC) at Penn State(WE ARE!), where I did my undergrad in Computer Science.

I am specifically interested in topics related to Computer Vision and Deep Learning.

Research
Non-trivial Image Similarity Detection

supervised by Svetlana Lazebnik, 2018 - present Demo Page coming soon

We try to teach DNN visual similarity with respect to different concepts and achieve image retrieval on Totally-Look-Like dataset accordingly.

Quaternion-based Keypose Analysis of Long 3D Activity Sequences

supervised by Robert Collins and Yanxi Liu, 2017-2018 Not Public Available Yet

Evaluate long (~5 mins) activity sequences in terms of performers' skill levels and the quality of their moves on Taiji.

Mutiple Object Tracking

supervised by Robert Collins, 2017-2018, Undergrad Thesis PDF

I invented a tracking algorithm to clean long-time raw motion capture sequences, which can speed up MoCap data cleaning process 20 times by leveraging the human skeleton dependency.
